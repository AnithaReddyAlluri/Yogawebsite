Yoga For Beginners

MountainSalute pose

How to do

Place your feet together and keep your arms by your sides. Make sure to plant all four corners of your feet firmly in the earth as you ground them. After that, extend your legs and contract your thigh muscles while tucking your tailbone in. Lengthen your torso and raise your arms before extending them out as you inhale. When you exhale, let go of your arms and move your shoulder blades back towards your waist and away from your head.

Benefits

The base stance for all standing poses, mountain pose enhances stability, confidence, groundedness, and posture. When performing Tadasana, deep breathing strengthens and expands the lungs.

CatPose

How to do

Begin on your hands and knees, keeping your spine neutral and your abs tight. After taking a deep breath, release your neck by rounding your spine upward towards the ceiling and tucking your chin into your chest. Relax your abs and arch your back on the subsequent breath. Raise your head and tailbone while taking care not to go too deeply or rapidly to avoid putting strain on your neck.

Benefits

The stretch enables the body to release tension or pain in the sciatica and lower back. increases adaptability. The neck, shoulders, and spine are more flexible in the cat cow stance. The muscles in the back, abdomen, chest, and hips are also stretched by this exercise.

childpose

How to do

To begin, bend over and tuck your toes under. With your arms outstretched, extend your upper body forward and downward, lowering your butt towards your feet. With your forehead on the mat, your stomach should be comfortably resting on your thigh

Benefits

Child's Pose can be a soothing and stress-relieving pose. This position deactivates the sympathetic nervous system and activates the parasympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for relaxing. This could aid in lowering or controlling blood pressure.

Downward facing pose

How to do

Get on your hands and knees, pointing your fingers forward and placing your palms just past your shoulder. Toes should be tucked in and knees should be beneath hips. Raise your hips and bring your body back into a V-shape. Place your feet hip-width apart. Remember, it's acceptable if your hamstrings are too tight to allow you to put your feet on the floor. Spread your ten fingers and toes, then extend your chest in the direction of your legs.

Benefits

strengthens the arms, shoulders, abdomen, legs, and upper body as a whole. stretches the spine, hamstrings, calves, ankles, and back of the body. calms the thoughts. stimulates the flow of blood.
