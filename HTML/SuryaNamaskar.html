SuryaNamaskar

Benefits of SuryaNamaskar

Surya Namaskar, or Sun Salutation, offers a comprehensive approach to physical and mental well-being. This sequence of yoga poses promotes flexibility, strength, and cardiovascular health, while also improving respiratory function and fostering stress reduction. It engages various muscle groups, enhances posture, and contributes to weight management. The synchronization of breath and movement in Surya Namaskar not only boosts energy levels but also cultivates mindfulness and a potential spiritual connection. Regular practice can lead to improved digestion, hormonal balance, and overall physical fitness, making it a holistic exercise for body and mind. Individuals with specific health concerns should seek guidance from healthcare professionals or experienced yoga instructors.

Pose1:Pranamasana(Prayer pose)

1.Maintain a parallel stance and distribute your weight equally across both feet.
2.Lie back on your shoulders and open up your chest.
3.Raise both arms from the sides as you inhale, and in the prayer position, bring the palms together in front of the chest as you release.

Pose2:Hastauttanasana(Raised arms pose)

1.Starting in tadasana, the yogi performs hasta uttanasana.
2.Next, raising both arms overhead so that the palms touch, tilt the head, neck, and upper body slightly back, and look up at the thumbs.

Pose3:Hasta Padasana(Hand to foot pose)

1.Exhale, then bend forward from the waist while maintaining a straight spine.
2.Let out a breath and fully lower your hands to the ground next to your feet.

Pose4:Ashwa Sanchalanasana(Equestrian pose)

1.Inhale and extend your right leg as far back as you can.
2.Lower your right leg to the ground and slowly raise your gaze.

Pose5:Dandasana(stick pose)

1.Inhale deeply and return your left leg.
2.Ensure that your entire body is erect.

Pose6:Ashtanga Namaskara(Salute with eight parts)

1.Exhale as you slowly lower your knees to the floor.
2.Slide forward and take your hips back a little bit.
3.Put your chin down and relax your chest.
4.Raise your rear end a little.

Pose7:Bhujangasana(Cobra pose)

1.In order to strike the Cobra stance, slide forward and lift your chest.
2.In this stance, maintain your elbows bent and fixed.
3.Avoid putting your shoulders next to your ears.
4.Look up slowly.

Pose8:Parvatasana(Mountain pose)

1.Exhale, raising your tailbone and your hips simultaneously.
2.You can make an inverted V pose by lowering your chest.

Pose9:Ashwa Sanchalanasana(Equestrian pose)

1.Inhale deeply and place your right foot forward between your palms.
2.Lower yourself to the floor with your left knee.
3.Look up after pressing your hips downward.

Pose10:Hasta Padasana(Hand to foot pose)

1.Exhale, then step forward with your left foot.
2.Place your hands flat on the ground.
3.If you are uncomfortable, you can bend your knees.

Pose11:Hastauttanasana(Raised Arms Pose)

1.Inhale and raise your spine.
2.Raise your hands and slightly slant your back.
3.Extrude your hips just a little bit.

Pose12:Tadasana(Standing Mountain pose)

1.First, exhale and straighten your posture.
2.Lower your arms.
